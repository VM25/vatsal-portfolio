# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Quant-finance recruiters and hiring managers evaluating Vatsal Maniar for 2026
internships and co-ops, across:

- front-office-adjacent quant (trading, research),
- derivatives risk, structuring, and capital-markets desks,
- fintech and quant-engineering teams.

Context: reviewing on desktop, often time-pressured and skimming many candidate
sites in a row, looking for concrete evidence of ability rather than claims.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio positioning Vatsal — M.S. Financial Engineering (Stevens),
B.S. Computer Science (Arizona State) — for quant finance, derivatives risk,
trading systems, and portfolio-analytics roles.

It is deliberately not a résumé page. It is a scroll-driven spatial experience
whose structure is itself evidence: moving through a financial-engineering
"system" (entry → academic origin → capability → project engines → execution
record → research direction → contact). Success is a recruiter leaving convinced
he builds real, applied quant systems — pricing, risk, market-making, portfolio
optimization — and remembering him among generic student portfolios.

## Brand Personality

Institutional, precise, cinematic. The voice is sharp, finance-aware, and
evidence-first: confident without overclaiming, serious but memorable. Motion is
controlled and purposeful rather than showy. It should read like market
infrastructure a desk would trust, not a template.

## Anti-references

- A horizontal scroll-corridor, sideways carousel, card-slider, or slide deck
  (the prior failure mode — vertical wheel must drive pinned in-place scenes).
- A cyan/teal SaaS / AI / fintech dark-mode template; grid-heavy generic look.
- Astronomy / outer-space / particle-field / solar-system visuals.
- Cyberpunk or neon styling; crypto landing-page aesthetics; gaming UIs.
- A normal vertical-scroll one-page grid-of-cards portfolio.
- The retired warm amber + serif editorial look used in earlier builds.
- Decorative shapes, particles, or abstract wireframes with no finance meaning.
- Fake Bloomberg-style dashboards or any fabricated metrics / performance numbers.
- Prompt-leak, placeholder, instructional, or apologetic copy.

## Design Principles

1. **Evidence over assertion.** The projects are the argument. Show working
   systems and real artifacts, not adjectives about ability.
2. **The experience is the proof.** Spatial, scroll-driven craft demonstrates
   the engineering skill the content claims; the medium backs the message.
3. **One connected story, no repetition.** Each scene answers a distinct
   question (who / how / what he can do / what he built / where he executed /
   where it's going / contact). A fact appears once, in its scene.
4. **Honest positioning.** A Financial Engineering student with a CS foundation
   and applied quant projects. Never imply he already holds a trader/quant-
   researcher role; frame non-finance experience as exactly what it was.
5. **Restraint as signal.** Institutional precision. Every element — including
   every 3D object — must be justifiable to the finance story, or it is cut.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG 2.1 AA: body text ≥4.5:1 on the warm graphite palette, visible
  brass focus rings, semantic HTML, and keyboard operability for all
  interactions (role selection, engine inspector, chapter navigation).
- Reduced motion: pinning and scrubbed choreography are bypassed in favor of an
  accessible, natively-scrollable vertical stack; all content stays readable and
  instruments render in their settled state.
- No 3D dependency: finance objects are crisp DOM/SVG instruments, not WebGL;
  nothing essential depends on a GPU canvas.
- Responsive: pinned cinematic chapters run on wide desktops (≥1024px); narrow
  and mobile viewports fall back to the stacked layout, with the role/engine
  rails becoming horizontally scrollable.
